Our Research Topic

Background

Sudden Unexpected Death in Infancy (SUDI) is a tragic and complex phenomenon that continues to impact families worldwide. While many deaths are preventable through safe sleep practices, not all families have equal access to the knowledge, resources, or culturally appropriate support to implement those practices.

This project investigates how technology, particularly a thoughtfully designed infant sleeping device, could support safer sleep environments across diverse communities.

Our Research Question

How can we improve the likelihood of various communities adopting this device, and what must be done to tailor it to their diverse needs and cultural practices?

Research Objectives

  • Understand cultural and practical factors influencing infant sleep practices.
  • Design a safe-sleep device adaptable to various community contexts.
  • Promote accessibility, affordability, and trust in our solution.
  • Integrate education with design to support informed decisions.

Why This Matters

Many SUDI-prevention tools and guidelines follow a one-size-fits-all model. Yet, families differ in living conditions, cultural norms, and their trust in healthcare or technology.

If we fail to design for inclusion and equity, we risk leaving behind the most vulnerable communities — the very families that may benefit the most from innovation and education.
