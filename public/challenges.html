Challenges

We face several critical challenges in our mission to develop a culturally respectful, widely adopted, and practical solution to prevent SUDI.

Cultural Differences in Infant Sleep Practices

  • Infant sleep traditions vary greatly across communities — from co-sleeping to cradle use.
  • Some families may resist devices that challenge generational knowledge or customs.
  • We must respect these differences while maintaining safety standards.

Stigma Around Medical or Assistive Devices

  • Parents may feel judged for needing a sleep-monitoring product.
  • Devices that appear too clinical could cause anxiety or feel out of place in the home.
  • We're committed to designing tools that feel supportive, not alarming.

Adoption & Usability Barriers

  • Affordability and access can be limiting, especially in underserved regions.
  • Complex or tech-heavy designs may overwhelm new parents.
  • Trust must be earned through thoughtful design and community endorsement.

Communication & Education Gaps

  • Many people still don’t understand what SUDI is or how it occurs.
  • Our messaging must be simple, consistent, and culturally sensitive.
  • Multilingual resources and accessible design are key to reaching all families.

Regulatory and Ethical Hurdles

  • If classified as a medical device, extra certifications may be needed.
  • Any data collected must be stored ethically and securely.
  • We must market ethically without exploiting fear.
